# Technique T0162.003: Historic Content Incorrectly Presented as Current

**Summary**: Video and Image content published or created in the past may be reshared and misrepresented as showing current events. For example, footage depicting previous armed conflicts have been reshared as depicting current conflicts.<br><br>To make this assertion, analysts need to identify the original source which a piece of content has come from, and confirm that it was published or created on a different date or time than it is being presented as depicting.<br><br>Indicators include discrepancies in timestamps or meta data, visible environmental or contextual clues (such as weather, attire, or signage), the absence of current corroborating reports, or analysts using tools such as reverse image search providers to identify previous publications of content.

**Tactic**: TA14 Develop Narratives <br><br>**Parent Technique:** T0162 Reframe Context
