# Incident I00135: Hamas Attacks, Israel Bombs Gaza and Misinformation Surges Online

* **Summary:** <i>Misinformation has been spreading rapidly online ever since Hamas launched a surprise attack on Israel on October 7. At least 1,000 people were killed and the Islamist militant group took between 100 and 150 hostages. Israel retaliated, threatening a complete siege of Gaza and hitting the area with multiple airstrikes, killing more than 900 people. <br><br>Alongside credible reports on the unfolding conflict, misinformation about incidents attributed to both sides have been widely circulated online. With the overwhelming amount of social media content on the conflict, it can be difficult to parse out verified information from posts that have been reused or manipulated.<br><br>In recent days, viral social media posts have presented years-old footage, or footage from entirely different conflict zones, as depicting the latest Israeli bombing of Gaza. They have baselessly claimed that a video of a lost girl in an Arabic-speaking country is an Israeli hostage in Gaza and have presented flares lit by Algerian football fans as the lights and explosions of a warzone. These are but a few examples.</i>
